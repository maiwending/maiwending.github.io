中英文语言与教育方式的差异

October 18, 2021

在美国宾州访学期间，很幸运认识一位朋友。他们家有两个女儿，一个儿子，年龄与我家土豆相仿。 有一次去朋友家做客，他5岁的女儿自己在小纸片上写字，偶尔有不会的单词问一问她老爸怎么拼。我十分好奇，就去看看她在小纸片上写的啥。

有很多张小纸片，每张小纸片上的内容结构相仿。但这样几个完整句子组成的一段话写自一个刚5岁的小女孩之手，实在是不能不让我震惊：

纸片一正面： I can swim. Guess what I am? 背面： I am a fish!

纸片二正面： I can fly. Guess what I am? 背面： I am a bird!

…… 诸如此类……

不同于国内家庭，把小孩送去各种早教班。国外的孩子环境十分宽松，每天下午三点就从学校放学，回到家中。那么他们怎么能够在如此幼小就有如此惊人的读写能力呢？

我觉得这是与语言分不开的。

英语作为表音文字，其学习曲线相当平缓。孩子们首先耳濡目染地学会了听、说。然后，通过字母歌等朗朗上口的儿歌，就可以掌握26个英语字母。通过将这26个英语字母，与平时耳濡目染的听和说学会的发音结合起来，就可以很平缓，很快乐地学会读和写。

而中文作为表意文字，学习曲线相当陡峭。我们没有ABC这样的基本单元。汉字的构成，尽管有部分可以通过象形文字来帮助记忆、理解，但绝大多数是要依靠一个一个汉字死记硬背的。由于儿童的成长曲线，7岁以前的儿童并不适宜死记硬背。所以中国的儿童学习读写，普遍要比英语国家的儿童晚（7岁VS5岁）。

文字读写能力直接影响后续的逻辑能力，其重要性我不用赘述。但是更重要的，是这种文字的不同，带来学习方式的差异。从而导致了中文语种与英文语种儿童在思维方式，学习方式的差异。

首先，平缓的学习曲线能够让英文儿童在轻松快乐的状态下学习。这种轻松快乐的氛围，赋予了儿童此后主动学习的自学能力。 其次，相对简单地读写包容了儿童的独立性。不同于家长式的教育，英文儿童不需要太多的“管教”，因此更加自由，更加独立。 然后，这样自由独立地学习读写也赋予了英文儿童的创造力。他们不需要去死记硬背很多内容；相反，他们允许通过字母的不同排列组合，去尝试造字。 最后，简单容易的读写也允许儿童的多样性。乐于学习的儿童可以自学地更多，志于服务、运动等其它多样性的儿童也可以以很少成本学习读写，并更多专注于自己的兴趣。

另一方面，中文教育当然也有其独特的特点。 首先，陡峭的学习曲线锻炼了中文儿童坚韧不拔的意志。我相信中国是全球加班情况最严峻的国家，没有之一。我们忍受了对汉字的死记硬背，也就能够忍受各种挑战。 其次，大量的中文字词训练了中文儿童超强的记忆力。 然后，中文的学习是痛苦的。为了克服这种痛苦而必须的“管教”，也教会了儿童顺服。 最后，中文是强类型语言，很多的多音字多意字，必须结合上下文加以理解。所以必须通过大量的阅读，才能够较全面地掌握中文语言。而大量的阅读也赋予中文学生丰富的知识。

遗憾的是，随着科技的发展，知识爆炸，各种搜索引擎能力空前，人工智能咄咄逼人。在这样的环境下，中文学生丰富的知识，超强的记忆力等等特点已经越来越容易被科技所取代。相反，英文儿童的自学能力，创造力却凸显出来，可以与科技相互互补，而体现出科技所不能达到的“人”的能力。

基于此，尽管我热爱中文，留恋中文诗词、古文的美。我仍不得不对中文的未来是报悲观态度的。

另外，认识到这两种语言带来教育方式的不同，我们就知道不能够脱离语言，盲目学习国外的教育方式。目前社会中一直倡导的减负，轻松自由的学习方式，在英文国家获得了巨大成功，但可能并不适用于中文环境。这不仅仅是由于中国的竞争激烈，高考、就业形势严峻等等……而是在中文陡峭的学习曲线面前，轻松自由只能是空闲时的调剂，而绝对不能成为主线。轻松自由的中文儿童，如果在陡峭的中文学习曲线面前败下阵来，会影响后续的阅读能力、逻辑能力与思维能力，是悲剧的。

最后，除了中英文的对比，日文中有部分汉字，德语号称最难的语言。所以这两者的学习曲线也较陡峭。因此日德方式的教育理念会更适宜借鉴，用于教育中文儿童。但是，日文的假名，德语都是表音文字，所以学习曲线相对于中文还是较为平缓的。

中文孩子们，奋斗吧！

During my visit to Pennsylvania, USA, I was fortunate to meet a friend. Their family has two daughters and one son, who is about the same age as our kids. Once when he went to a friend’s house as a guest, his 5-year-old daughter wrote on small pieces of paper by herself, and occasionally asked her dad how to spell a word she couldn’t know. I was very curious, so I went to see what she wrote on the small piece of paper.

There are many small pieces of paper, and the content structure on each small piece of paper is similar. But such a paragraph composed of several complete sentences was written by a little girl who was just 5 years old, which really shocked me:

The first side of the paper: I can swim. Guess what I am? back: I am a fish!

Front side of paper two: I can fly. Guess what I am? back: I am a bird!

… And so on…

Unlike Chinese children who are usually sent out to various early education classes. The environment for children abroad is very relaxed, leaving school at 3 o’clock every afternoon and returning home. So how can they have such amazing literacy skills at such a young age?

I think at least part of this comes from the different language.

The learning curve of English as phonetic writing is quite gentle. The children first learned to listen and speak naturally. Then, through catchy children’s songs such as alphabet songs, one can master 26 English alphabets. By combining these 26 English letters with the pronunciation of listening and speaking, you can learn how to read and write very smoothly and happily.

As for Chinese as an ideogram, the learning curve is quite steep. We don’t have a basic unit like ABC. Although part of the composition of Chinese characters can be memorized and understood through pictographs, most of them rely on rote memorization of Chinese characters one by one. Due to the growth curve of children, children before the age of 7 are not suitable for rote memorization. Therefore, Chinese children generally learn to read and write later than children in English-speaking countries (7 years old vs. 5 years old).

The literacy difference directly affects the follow-up logical development. But more importantly, this difference in language brings about differences in learning methods. This leads to differences in the thinking and learning styles of Chinese and English children.

First of all, the gentle learning curve allows English children to learn in a relaxed and happy state. This relaxed and happy atmosphere gives children the self-learning ability to learn actively in the future. Secondly, the relatively simple reading and writing tolerates children’s independence. Unlike paternalistic education, English children do not need too much “discipline”, so they are more free and independent. Then, learning to read and write freely and independently in this way also gives English children creativity. They don’t need to memorize a lot of content; on the contrary, they allow different permutations and combinations of letters to try to make characters based on their own understanding. Finally, simple and easy reading and writing also allows children’s diversity. Children who like academics can learn more on their own, and children who commit themselves to service, sports, musics and others can also learn basic reading and writing at a small cost and focus themselves more on their own interests.

On the other hand, Chinese education certainly has its own unique characteristics. First of all, the steep learning curve has tempered the Chinese children’s perseverance. I believe that China is the country with the most severe overtime working in the world. This does not come with no reason. If we can endure the rote memorization of Chinese characters, we can endure nearly all kinds of challenges. Secondly, a large number of Chinese words have trained Chinese children with super memory. Then, Chinese learning is painful. So the “discipline” is necessary to overcome this pain. Finally, Chinese is a strongly typed language. Many polyphonic and multi-meaning characters must be understood in context. Therefore, a lot of reading is necessary to be able to master the Chinese language more comprehensively. And a lot of reading also gives Chinese students rich knowledge.

Unfortunately, with the development of science and technology, the explosion of knowledge, the unprecedented capabilities of various search engines and artificial intelligence. In such an environment, Chinese students’ rich knowledge, super memory and other characteristics that were advantageous have now become more and more easily replaced by technology. On the contrary, the self-learning ability and creativity of English children are highlighted, as that reflects the unique ability of “human being” that science and technology cannot achieve.

In addition, recognizing the difference in educational methods brought about by these two languages. The relaxed and free learning method has been a great success in English-speaking countries. But it may not be suitable for the Chinese-speaking environment. This is not only due to the fierce competition in China, the college entrance examination, the severe employment situation, etc….but also because of the language. In the face of the steep learning curve of Chinese, ease and free education can be casual complement, but absolutely cannot be the main line. If the relaxed Chinese children fail in the face of the steep Chinese learning curve, it will affect their subsequent reading ability, logical ability and thinking ability, which is tragic.

Finally, in addition to the comparison between Chinese and English, there are some Chinese characters in Japanese, and Germany is also known as one of the most difficult languages. So the learning curve for the Japanese and Germany is also steeper. Therefore, the Japanese-German educational concept might be more transformable for Chinese-speaking education. However, Japanese kana and German are phonetic characters, so the learning curve is still relatively more smooth that of Chinese.
