abstract: 'Planar periodic structures are widely utilized in microwave applications. The prism-based Discontinuous Galerkin time domain (DGTD) method is optimal to cope with the modeling challenges associated with these planar structures. In this work, we modified the prism based DGTD to take lossy materials into account. A ring-shaped frequency selective surface (FSS) is studied as a representative numerical example. When submerged into water, the operating frequency of the FSS is lowered dramatically. We test the algorithm with distilled and tap water of different conductivity.
